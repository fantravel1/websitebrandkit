Fitness Branding Tips

üí° Pro Tip: Energy Over Everything

Your brand should motivate and energize. Bold colors and action-oriented emojis inspire people to move and get fit.

Color Psychology for Fitness

  • Red: Energy, power, intensity (CrossFit, F45, Barry's Bootcamp)
  • Green: Health, balance, natural (Equinox, yoga studios)
  • Orange: Enthusiasm, fun, accessible (Orangetheory, Crunch)
  • Black: Premium, serious, elite (Peloton, SoulCycle)
  • Blue: Trust, stability, professional (24 Hour Fitness, fitness apps)
  • Purple: Creative, boutique, feminine (Pure Barre, women's fitness)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Passive imagery: Use action emojis (runner, lifter) not static ones
  2. Soft colors: Avoid pastels - fitness needs energy and boldness
  3. Too many elements: One powerful emoji beats multiple weak ones
  4. Unclear niche: Specify your fitness type (yoga vs weightlifting)
  5. Desktop-only thinking: Most clients book on mobile - test there first
