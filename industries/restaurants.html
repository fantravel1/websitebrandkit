Restaurant Branding Tips

üí° Pro Tip: Match Your Food Type

Your logo should instantly tell customers what type of food you serve. A pizza emoji is clearer than a generic fork and knife.

Color Psychology for Food

  • Red & Yellow: Stimulates appetite, creates urgency (McDonald's, KFC, In-N-Out)
  • Green: Health, freshness, organic (Sweetgreen, Whole Foods, Subway)
  • Brown & Beige: Comfort, warmth, artisanal (Starbucks, Panera)
  • Orange: Energy, affordability, fun (Dunkin', Hooters)
  • Black & Gold: Premium, sophisticated (Nobu, high-end steakhouses)
  • Pink: Sweet, playful, Instagram-friendly (dessert shops, bubble tea)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Generic symbols: Avoid fork/knife - use your actual food item
  2. Cool colors: Blue suppresses appetite - avoid for most restaurants
  3. Too complex: Simple emoji > elaborate illustrations for social media
  4. Wrong emoji tone: Make sure emoji style matches on all devices
  5. Clashing with food photos: Test logo against your actual menu items
