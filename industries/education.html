Education Branding Tips

üí° Pro Tip: Build Trust with Parents

Your brand needs to appeal to both students AND parents. Professional colors build parental trust while fun emojis engage students.

Color Psychology for Education

  • Blue: Trust, intelligence, stability (Harvard, Yale, most universities)
  • Green: Growth, harmony, learning (Khan Academy, Duolingo)
  • Orange: Creativity, enthusiasm, accessibility (Coursera, edX)
  • Purple: Wisdom, imagination, premium (Udemy, creative schools)
  • Navy/Gold: Prestige, tradition, excellence (Oxford, Cambridge)
  • Red: Energy, passion, determination (language learning, bootcamps)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Too playful for serious subjects: Balance fun with professionalism
  2. Outdated imagery: Avoid old-school chalkboard aesthetics unless retro is your brand
  3. Unclear audience: Specify age group (K-12, college, adult learning)
  4. Ignoring mobile: Parents research schools on phones - test logo visibility
  5. Generic symbols: Use subject-specific emojis (microscope for science, not just books)
