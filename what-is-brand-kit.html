What is a Brand Kit?

A brand kit (also called a brand identity kit or branding kit) is a collection of visual assets and guidelines that define your brand's identity. Think of it as your brand's visual DNA - everything someone needs to represent your brand consistently and professionally.

Brand Kit Definition

A brand kit is a comprehensive package containing your logo, color palette, typography, icons, favicons, social media images, and usage guidelines that ensure visual consistency across all platforms.

What is a Website Branding Kit?

A website branding kit specifically includes all the visual assets needed to brand your website and online presence. It's more focused than a general brand kit, concentrating on digital assets.

Essential Website Branding Kit Assets

  1. Favicons (Browser Tab Icons)
    16x16px, 32x32px, and 48x48px PNG files plus a multi-size .ICO file. These appear in browser tabs and bookmarks.
  2. Apple Touch Icon
    180x180px PNG that displays when iOS users add your site to their home screen.
  3. Android Chrome Icons
    192x192px and 512x512px PNG files for Android devices and PWA (Progressive Web App) support.
  4. Open Graph Image
    1200x630px image that appears when your link is shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, and other platforms.
  5. Twitter Card Image
    1200x600px image optimized for Twitter/X link previews.
  6. Brand Colors
    Primary and secondary colors in HEX format for consistent styling across your website.

How to Create Branding for a Brand New Website

Launching a brand new website? Here's how to create professional branding from scratch:

  1. Define Your Brand Identity
    What does your brand represent? Choose 3-5 words that describe your brand personality (professional, creative, friendly, innovative, etc.)
  2. Choose Your Brand Colors
    Select a primary color based on color psychology. Blue suggests trust, green represents growth, orange conveys creativity. Add 1-2 complementary accent colors.
  3. Select Your Brand Icon or Emoji
    Pick a symbol that represents your brand's purpose. A rocket for startups, a lightbulb for ideas, a target for goals. This will become your favicon and visual identifier.
  4. Generate Your Brand Assets
    Use a free brand kit generator to create all required favicon sizes, social images, and mobile icons in your brand colors.
  5. Implement on Your Website
    Upload favicons to your root directory and add the proper HTML meta tags. Set up Open Graph and Twitter Card images for social sharing.
  6. Extend to Social Profiles
    Use the same colors and icon across all your social media profiles for consistent brand recognition.
