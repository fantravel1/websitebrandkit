How do I create brand emojis?

+

To create brand emojis, follow these 5 steps:

  • Define your core value: Identify the ONE word that represents your brand (speed, growth, trust, creativity, etc.)
  • Choose your emoji: Select an emoji that represents that value and fits your industry
  • Select brand colors: Pick 2-3 complementary colors based on color psychology
  • Generate assets: Use a free branded emoji generator to create favicons, social images, and icons

The entire process takes under 5 minutes with no design skills required.

How many emojis should my brand use?

+

For maximum brand recognition, use 1-2 core emojis:

  • Primary emoji: Your main brand identifier used on all major touchpoints (favicon, social profiles, email)
  • Secondary emoji: Optional, for specific campaigns or content categories

Using more than 2 core emojis dilutes recognition. Consistency is more important than variety in emoji branding. Focus on using the same emoji everywhere rather than using different emojis for different purposes.

Brand Color Meanings

What does blue mean in branding?

+

Blue is the most popular brand color, used by 33% of top 100 brands. Blue brand color meaning includes:

  • Trust - People feel secure with blue brands
  • Reliability - Suggests dependability
  • Professionalism - Corporate and business-appropriate
  • Security - Financial and tech associations
  • Intelligence - Calms the mind, promotes productivity

Best for: Technology (Facebook, LinkedIn), Finance (PayPal), Healthcare, Corporate services

Avoid for: Food brands (blue suppresses appetite)

What does red mean in branding?

+

Red brand color meaning includes:

  • Energy - Creates excitement and action
  • Passion - Emotional and intense
  • Urgency - Perfect for sales and CTAs
  • Appetite - Stimulates hunger (great for food)
  • Power - Bold and confident

Best for: Food & Beverage (Coca-Cola, McDonald's), Entertainment (Netflix, YouTube), Retail (Target), Sports

Tip: Use sparingly; too much red can feel aggressive

What does green mean in branding?

Green brand color meaning includes:

How do I choose the right brand colors?

+

Follow these 7 steps to choose brand colors:

  • 1. Define brand personality: What 3-5 words describe your brand?
  • 2. Research your audience: What colors appeal to your target demographics?
  • 3. Analyze competitors: What colors do they use? How can you differentiate?
  • 4. Study color psychology: What emotions does each color evoke?
  • 5. Choose primary color: Pick one color that represents your main value
  • 6. Add accent colors: Select 1-2 complementary colors
  • 7. Test and iterate: Get feedback and refine

How do I add branded emoji favicons to my website?

+

Add these HTML tags in your <head> section:

<link rel="icon" type="image/png" sizes="32x32" href="/favicon-32x32.png">
<link rel="icon" type="image/png" sizes="16x16" href="/favicon-16x16.png">
<link rel="apple-touch-icon" sizes="180x180" href="/apple-touch-icon.png">
<link rel="manifest" href="/site.webmanifest">
<meta property="og:image" content="https://yoursite.com/og-image.jpg">
<meta name="twitter:image" content="https://yoursite.com/twitter-card.jpg">

Upload all favicon files to your website's root directory.

Business & Legal

Can I legally use emojis as my business logo?

+

Yes, you can legally use Unicode emojis as your business logo or brand identifier. Emojis are free to use commercially as they are universal communication symbols defined by the Unicode Consortium.

However, note that:

  • Specific emoji designs may be copyrighted by platforms (Apple, Google)
  • You're using the concept of the emoji, which displays in each platform's native style
  • This is similar to using a font - the letter "A" is universal, but each font renders it differently
